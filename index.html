Pillars of " Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

As Bangladesh stands at the crossroads of its development journey, the nation is poised to enter what many call "Bangladesh 2.0."

Sustainability

Bangladesh 2.0 encompasses various strategic initiatives, from economic overhauls and infrastructural advancements to social reforms and environmental sustainability.

Infrastructure

The government has already taken steps in this direction, such as promoting renewable energy, improving disaster preparedness,and implementing policies to reduce carbon emissions.

Empowerment

The country has invested significantly in mega-projects such as the Padma Bridge, the Dhaka Metro Rail, and the Payra Deep Sea Port.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

A vision of future digital transformation envisions a world where technology seamlessly integrates into every aspect of life,enabling hyper-personalized experiences,highly automated processes,data-driven decision making,and a focus on sustainability.

Renewable Energy

A vision of future renewable energy sees a world where the majority of electricity is generated from sources like solar, wind, geothermal, and hydropower.

Robust Infrastructure

A vision of future robust infrastructure would encompass highly adaptable, resilient systems that seamlessly integrate advanced technology, prioritizing sustainability, equitable access, and intelligent management.

The "country as a family" metaphor, while evocative, may not fully capture the diverse realities and challenges facing the nation. Unity is essential, but it must also respect and embrace diversity. Citizens are not just looking for unity; they want a sense of autonomy and inclusivity within the broader national framework.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
